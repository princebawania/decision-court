# Decision Memo: Should a small D2C brand invest its marketing budget in Instagram influencers or in Google performance ads?

**Verdict: OPTION A: invest its marketing budget in Instagram influencers**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. Evidence from Influencer Marketing Hub and case studies support the potential for high ROI in influencer marketing
2. The fact-check results verify the claims made about influencer marketing, including the potential for $6.50 ROI for every dollar spent and over 8 billion views on a single UGC campaign
3. The unverified claims made about Google Ads, such as its market share and potential revenue, undermine the credibility of the argument for OPTION B

## This verdict flips if
- Google Ads provides concrete, verifiable evidence of its market share and potential revenue
- Influencer marketing is proven to be less scalable or reliable for the average D2C brand

## The case that was argued

### For: invest its marketing budget in Instagram influencers
Investing in Instagram influencers can provide a small D2C brand with measurable ROI and brand building on a limited budget. According to various studies, influencer marketing has shown significant growth and ROI, with some brands achieving up to 8 billion views on their campaigns. By partnering with the right influencers, D2C brands can reach their target audience, build brand awareness, and drive sales. For instance, a case study by Influencer Hero Platform reported a D2C brand scaling from $0 to $1.2M in revenue within one year using influencer marketing.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While the opponent's claims may seem impressive, they rely heavily on isolated success stories and lack concrete evidence of long-term sustainability. The fact that these results are achievable by only a select few raises questions about the scalability and reliability of influencer marketing for the average D2C brand. Furthermore, the lack of control over the messaging and audience engagement in influencer marketing can lead to inconsistent brand representation and unpredictable outcomes.

### For: invest its marketing budget in Google performance ads
Investing in Google performance ads is the best option for a small D2C brand with a limited marketing budget, as it offers measurable ROI and brand building opportunities. With over 96% of brands investing in Google Ads, it's clear that this platform provides effective results. According to various case studies, Google Ads can generate significant revenue, such as turning a ₹10,000 budget into ₹1.2 lakh in revenue. Additionally, Google Ads dominates the PPC market with an 80% market share, making it a reliable choice for small businesses.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** My opponent's argument relies on unverified claims, including Google Ads' market share and the potential to turn ₹10,000 into ₹1.2 lakh in revenue. These unsubstantiated assertions undermine the credibility of their argument. Furthermore, the fact that 96% of brands invest in Google Ads doesn't necessarily mean it's the best choice for a small D2C brand with limited budget. Blindly following the crowd is not a marketing strategy.
