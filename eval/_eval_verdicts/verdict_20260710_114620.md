# Decision Memo: Should I just use Uber daily, or buy a car?

**Verdict: use Uber daily**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. total cost of owning a car includes expenses such as maintenance, insurance, and fuel, which can be costly
2. ride-hailing services provide safety, comfort, and a fast payment process, making them a preferred choice for many
3. using ride-hailing instead of owning a vehicle could reduce overall vehicle use and promote sustainable mobility

## This verdict flips if
- you plan to drive extensively and can realize long-term cost savings through car ownership
- you live in an area with limited Uber availability or frequent surge pricing

---

## The case that was argued

### For: use Uber daily
Using Uber daily is a cost-effective, flexible, and convenient option. According to Rocket Money, owning a car comes with various expenses, whereas using Uber can be more budget-friendly. Additionally, ride-hailing services provide safety, comfort, and a fast payment process, making them a preferred choice for many. With the rise of urban transportation, using ride-hailing instead of owning a vehicle could reduce overall vehicle use and promote sustainable mobility.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent touts the benefits of using Uber daily, they overlook the lack of personal freedom and spontaneity that comes with relying on ride-hailing services. Moreover, their argument hinges on cost-effectiveness, but what about areas with limited Uber availability or surge pricing? The convenience and flexibility they claim are not universally applicable, making car ownership a more practical choice for many individuals.

### For: buy a car
Buying a car provides flexibility, convenience, and can be cost-effective in the long run. According to GeraRide, the total cost of owning a car should be considered, not just petrol costs. Moore's Autos and rideculturedaily.com highlight the numerous benefits of car ownership, including enhanced lifestyle and practical transportation solutions. With the ability to travel freely and the potential for long-term cost savings, buying a car is a viable option.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent cites various sources, they overlook the significant upfront costs and maintenance responsibilities associated with car ownership. The benefits of flexibility and enhanced lifestyle come with a hefty price tag, including insurance, parking, and potential traffic woes. Furthermore, the potential for long-term cost savings is highly dependent on usage, and many individuals may not drive enough to realize these savings, making daily Uber use a more practical and hassle-free option.
