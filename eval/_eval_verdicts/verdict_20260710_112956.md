# Decision Memo: Should a small D2C brand spend its budget on Instagram influencers, or on Google performance ads?

**Verdict: spend its budget on Google performance ads**
**Confidence: 85%**

## Why
1. Google Ads has a supported 80% market share in the PPC market
2. Google Ads offers comprehensive benchmarks and performance data to optimize campaigns
3. the claims made in favor of Instagram influencers are either unverified or contradicted

## This verdict flips if
- new evidence emerges that supports the effectiveness of Instagram influencers for brand building on a small budget
- the brand's target audience is found to be more active on Instagram than on Google

---

## The case that was argued

### For: spend its budget on Instagram influencers
Spending the budget on Instagram influencers can be an effective way to achieve measurable ROI and brand building. According to the Aspire 2026 Influencer Marketing Report, influencer content is a primary driver of product discovery. Additionally, benchmark reports estimate that brands earn around $5.78 in revenue for every $1 spent on influencer marketing campaigns. This suggests that influencer marketing can provide a strong return on investment. By partnering with influencers who align with the brand's values, the brand can increase awareness and reach its target audience.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** The opponent's argument relies on unverified and contradicted claims. The assertion that influencer content drives product discovery is unsubstantiated, particularly on platforms like TikTok Shop. Furthermore, the claimed engagement rates for influencer marketing campaigns are directly contradicted by evidence, which suggests nano-influencers achieve significantly higher engagement rates than stated. These discrepancies undermine the credibility of the opponent's argument, casting doubt on the effectiveness of spending the budget on Instagram influencers.

### For: spend its budget on Google performance ads
Given the brand's priorities of measurable ROI and brand building on a small budget, allocating the budget to Google performance ads is the most effective strategy. According to the gathered web evidence, Google Ads dominates the PPC market with an 80% market share and offers comprehensive benchmarks and performance data to optimize campaigns. By setting an average daily budget and utilizing Google Ads' features, the brand can effectively manage its budget and achieve its marketing goals. 

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While Google Ads may dominate the PPC market, its effectiveness for brand building is questionable. The opponent's argument relies heavily on measurable ROI, but neglects to consider the brand's need for creative storytelling and community engagement. Instagram influencers can provide a more holistic approach to marketing, fostering brand loyalty and awareness in a way that Google Ads cannot. By prioritizing Google Ads, the brand may be sacrificing long-term brand growth for short-term conversions.
