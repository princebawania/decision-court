# Decision Memo: Should I just use Uber daily, or buy a car?

**Verdict: buy a car**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. the true monthly cost of car ownership can be lower than using Uber daily for frequent drivers
2. buying a car provides flexibility and convenience that ride-hailing services cannot match
3. the break-even mileage for owning a car vs using ride-hailing services can be determined using the Ride Hailing vs Car Ownership Calculator

## This verdict flips if
- the decision-maker's daily trips are very short and infrequent
- the decision-maker values the convenience of door-to-door service without parking and maintenance over the flexibility of owning a car

---

## The case that was argued

### For: use Uber daily
Using Uber daily is a cost-effective, flexible, and convenient option. According to various sources, including a Reddit discussion and a calculator from Urbanism Next, the true cost of vehicle ownership can be compared to the cost of using ride-hailing services. On average, an Uber trip is priced at a certain amount, and with the help of tools like the Uber Fare Calculator, users can estimate their costs. This flexibility and transparency make Uber a viable option for daily transportation.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent touts the flexibility of Uber, they overlook the lack of control and personal space that comes with it. Moreover, their argument relies heavily on averages and estimates, which may not reflect individual circumstances. Notably, they fail to address the limitations and potential biases of the Urbanism Next calculator and Reddit discussion they cite, which may not account for factors like parking, tolls, and the value of time spent in traffic.

### For: buy a car
Buying a car provides flexibility and convenience that ride-hailing services cannot match, and while the costs may seem similar, owning a car can be more cost-effective in the long run, especially for those who drive frequently. According to the Free Rideshare vs. Car Ownership Calculator, the true monthly cost of car ownership includes payment, insurance, fuel, maintenance, and depreciation, which can be compared to the cost of using ride-hailing services. Ultimately, the decision to buy a car or use Uber daily depends on individual circumstances, but for those who value flexibility and convenience, buying a car is the better option.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** My opponent's argument relies on flawed assumptions about individual circumstances and driving frequencies. They fail to consider the significant upfront costs of buying a car, such as down payments and registration fees. Additionally, their claims about flexibility and convenience are overstated, as Uber provides door-to-door service without the hassle of parking and maintenance. The calculators they cite only provide a partial view of the costs, ignoring the value of time and convenience that Uber offers.
