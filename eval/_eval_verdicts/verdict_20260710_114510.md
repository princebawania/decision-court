# Decision Memo: Should a small D2C brand spend its budget on Google performance ads, or on Instagram influencers?

**Verdict: spend its budget on Google performance ads**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. Google Ads provides measurable ROI through trackable conversions and revenue
2. integrating Google Ads with Google Analytics offers deeper insights for smarter decision-making
3. a higher Quality Score in Google Ads leads to better ad placements and lower costs

## This verdict flips if
- influencer marketing statistics provide verified evidence of a significant shift in budget allocation
- the small D2C brand has a strong existing presence on Instagram that can be leveraged for influencer marketing

---

## The case that was argued

### For: spend its budget on Google performance ads
Spending the budget on Google performance ads is the most effective way for a small D2C brand to achieve measurable ROI and brand building. According to the Google Ads Performance Grader, a thorough audit of the Google Ads account can be performed in 60 seconds or less, allowing for quick optimization. Additionally, integrating Google Ads with Google Analytics can offer deeper insights and smarter decision-making tools, as seen on TikTok. With a limited budget, every dollar needs to count, and Google Ads can provide better ad placements and lower costs with a higher Quality Score.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent touts Google Ads' benefits, they overlook its limitations for a small D2C brand. Notably, they mention TikTok, which is unrelated to Google Ads, revealing a lack of focus on the actual platform. Furthermore, their argument relies heavily on technical optimization, neglecting the creative and engaging aspects of marketing that Instagram influencers can provide, such as building brand awareness and community.

### For: spend its budget on Instagram influencers
Spending the budget on Instagram influencers is a strategic decision that can drive measurable ROI and brand building for the small D2C brand. According to influencer marketing statistics, many brands are shifting their budget away from traditional channels to influencer marketing, which can provide a cost-effective avenue for advertising. As seen in the case of a D2C brand that scaled from $0 to $1.2M in revenue in one year, influencer marketing can be a key channel for growth. By collaborating with influencers, businesses can gain access to unique, high-quality content that can be repurposed across their own marketing channels, increasing awareness, credibility, and community engagement.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent cites a successful case study, their broader claims about the shift to influencer marketing are unverified. The assertion that many brands are allocating 7.7% of revenue to influencer marketing lacks evidence, undermining the argument's foundation. Furthermore, relying on a single case study ignores the potential variability in influencer marketing's effectiveness across different brands and industries. This lack of concrete data raises questions about the strategy's scalability and reliability for the small D2C brand in question.
