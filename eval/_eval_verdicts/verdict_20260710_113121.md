# Decision Memo: Should I buy a car, or just use Uber daily?

**Verdict: use Uber daily**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. The average new car payment is $748 per month, which is a significant upfront cost
2. The average cost of an Uber trip is around $25, which can be more cost-effective for individuals with low to moderate travel needs
3. The evidence provided does not strongly support the claim that owning a car is more cost-effective and convenient for individuals with predictable commuting patterns

## This verdict flips if
- you have a large family or frequently need to transport multiple passengers or large items
- you live in an area with limited or unreliable ride-hailing services

---

## The case that was argued

### For: buy a car
Buying a car can be more cost-effective and convenient in the long run, especially for individuals with predictable commuting patterns and frequent travel needs. According to various studies, the fixed costs of car ownership, such as loan payments, insurance, and maintenance, can be outweighed by the benefits of flexibility and convenience. As seen in the cost comparison by Ridewise, a typical $25 rideshare fare can be more expensive than the monthly cost-per-trip math of owning a car. Additionally, having a car provides the freedom to travel at any time, without being dependent on ride-hailing services.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** My opponent's argument relies on unverified claims and flawed comparisons. The assertion that a $25 rideshare fare is more expensive than car ownership is unsubstantiated, and the evidence provided does not support the claim that owning a car is more cost-effective for individuals with predictable commuting patterns. Furthermore, the fact that the average new car payment is $748 per month highlights the significant upfront costs of car ownership, which can be a major burden for many individuals.

### For: just use Uber daily
Using Uber daily is a cost-effective, flexible, and convenient option. According to various studies, the costs of ride-hailing can be lower than owning a car, especially when considering the expenses of car maintenance, insurance, and fuel. With the average cost of an Uber trip being relatively low, relying on ride-hailing services can be a smart choice for those who value flexibility and convenience. As seen in the provided web evidence, calculators and analyses have been developed to help individuals determine whether using Uber is cheaper than owning a car, making it easier to make an informed decision.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** The opponent's argument relies on unverified claims about car ownership costs, lacking concrete evidence to support the $5000 estimate. This oversight undermines their entire cost comparison. Furthermore, their calculation only considers the cost per trip, neglecting the value of car ownership, such as flexibility to travel at any time and the ability to transport multiple passengers or large items, which Uber cannot match.
