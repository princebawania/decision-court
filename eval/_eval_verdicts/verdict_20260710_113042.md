# Decision Memo: Should a small D2C brand spend its budget on Google performance ads, or on Instagram influencers?

**Verdict: spend its budget on Instagram influencers**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. The fact-checked results support the claim that Instagram influencer collaborations deliver a higher return on investment ($5.78 per dollar invested) compared to paid ads ($2 for every $1 spent)
2. Influencer marketing campaigns can cater to niche audiences and targeted marketing opportunities, aligning with the brand's priority of measurable ROI and brand building on a small budget
3. The unverified claim about Google Ads' ROI and the lack of concrete evidence raise doubts about its effectiveness

## This verdict flips if
- verified evidence shows Google Ads providing a significantly higher ROI than influencer marketing
- the brand's target audience is not active on Instagram or does not respond well to influencer marketing

---

## The case that was argued

### For: spend its budget on Google performance ads
Spending the budget on Google performance ads is the most effective way to allocate resources for measurable ROI and brand building. According to marketing ROI statistics, Google Ads generate significant returns, with 61.9% of clicks coming from mobile devices and 76% of people searching on smartphones for something visiting a related store within a day. By creating specific campaigns and choosing the right bid and budget, the brand can maximize its Google Ads budget and achieve a high ROI.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent cites impressive mobile engagement statistics, their claim about Google Ads' ROI is unverified and lacks concrete evidence. The mention of "100 statistics" in the Google Ads ROI report is unsubstantiated, raising doubts about the actual returns on investment. Relying on unverified data to justify budget allocation is misleading and may lead to ineffective marketing strategies.

### For: spend its budget on Instagram influencers
Given the limited budget, spending on Instagram influencers is the most effective way to allocate resources for measurable ROI and brand building. According to recent statistics, influencer marketing delivers a higher return on investment compared to paid ads. With the right influencers, a brand can reach a targeted audience, increasing the likelihood of conversion and brand loyalty. As evidenced by various studies, influencer marketing can provide a significant return on investment, making it an attractive option for small D2C brands.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent cites impressive returns on Instagram influencer collaborations, they overlook the lack of control and transparency in influencer marketing. The fact that other sources provide conflicting information on paid ads' ROI undermines their claim about paid ads' ineffectiveness. Moreover, influencer marketing's reliance on individual influencers' audiences can be unpredictable and difficult to scale, making it a risky investment for limited budgets.
