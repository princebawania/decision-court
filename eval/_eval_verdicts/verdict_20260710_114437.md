# Decision Memo: Should a small D2C brand spend its budget on Instagram influencers, or on Google performance ads?

**Verdict: spend its budget on Google performance ads**
**Confidence: 80%**

## Why
1. Google Ads has a supported 80% market share in the PPC market
2. small businesses can achieve sustainable results with Google Ads on a modest budget
3. the claim of higher ROI for Google Ads is more plausible despite lacking direct evidence from Rudys.AI

## This verdict flips if
- Instagram provides concrete evidence of its ROI and lead generation rates
- the brand's target audience is predominantly on Instagram and not on Google

---

## The case that was argued

### For: spend its budget on Instagram influencers
Spending the budget on Instagram influencers is a strategic decision that can yield measurable ROI and effective brand building on a small budget. According to Sprout Social, Instagram has a significant audience and engagement potential. With 40% of all influencer campaigns on Instagram, as reported by Collabstr, it's a prime platform for influencer marketing. By partnering with influencers who create decent content on Reels, as suggested on Reddit, the brand can acquire paid usage rights and maximize its reach.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** My opponent's argument relies on unverified claims about Instagram's ROI and lead generation rates, lacking concrete evidence. Furthermore, they overlook the significantly higher engagement rate of 18% on TikTok compared to Instagram's 2.4%, which undermines their platform choice. With vague assertions and a failure to consider alternative platforms, their case for spending the budget on Instagram influencers is weakened, casting doubt on the effectiveness of their proposed strategy.

### For: spend its budget on Google performance ads
Given the brand's priorities of measurable ROI and brand building on a small budget, allocating the budget to Google performance ads is the most effective strategy. According to Google Ads statistics, Google Ads dominates the PPC market with an 80% market share, indicating its potential for high ROI. Additionally, with the right strategies, small businesses can compete and thrive with low-cost Google Ads, maximizing ROI with limited spending. This is supported by various expert analyses and benchmarks that provide insights into optimizing Google Ads campaigns for small budgets.

**Rebuttal from the other side:** While my opponent touts Google Ads' dominance, they rely on unverified claims about its ROI superiority. The cited source, Rudys.AI, lacks concrete evidence to support this assertion. Furthermore, their argument overlooks the limitations of Google Ads in building brand awareness, a crucial aspect of our brand's priorities. By focusing solely on performance ads, they neglect the importance of creative storytelling and community engagement that Instagram influencers can provide, making their strategy one-dimensional and incomplete.
